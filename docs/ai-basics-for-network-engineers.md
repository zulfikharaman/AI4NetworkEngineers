# AI Basics for Network Engineers

This section explains AI concepts using networking analogies.
No math. No coding. Only what matters for daily network work.

---

## What Is AI (In Simple Terms)?

AI is software that analyzes large amounts of text and patterns
and generates responses based on probability—not understanding.

Think of it as a very fast junior engineer
that has read thousands of documents.

---

## What Is Generative AI / LLM?

A Large Language Model (LLM):
- Does NOT think
- Does NOT know your network
- Predicts the next most likely words

It sounds confident even when it is wrong.

---

## What AI Is Good At

- Explaining CLI output
- Summarizing logs
- Suggesting troubleshooting directions
- Creating documentation drafts

---

## What AI Is BAD At

- Understanding your topology
- Knowing change windows
- Making safe production decisions
- Distinguishing lab vs prod

This is called **hallucination**.

---

## Why Verification Is Mandatory

AI suggestions must always be validated using:
- standard network troubleshooting steps
- vendor documentation
- lab testing

AI is an assistant, not an operator.
